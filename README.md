## Exam AB-731: AI Transformation Leader – Skills Measured

The exam is divided into three major domains, each focused on real-world leadership decisions and AI adoption challenges.

### 1. Identify the Business Value of Generative AI Solutions (35–40%)

This section focuses on understanding generative AI at a strategic level and knowing when—and when not—to use it.

**Key Topics Include:**

- Foundational concepts of generative AI
- Differences between generative AI and traditional AI
- Selecting the right generative AI solution for a business need
- Understanding pretrained vs. fine-tuned models
- Cost drivers such as tokens, usage patterns, and ROI
- Challenges including hallucinations, bias, reliability, and trust
- Identifying scenarios where generative AI delivers scalability, automation, and efficiency

You’re expected to evaluate business readiness, not just AI capability.

### 2. Identify Benefits and Capabilities of Microsoft’s AI Apps and Services (35–40%)

This domain is heavily focused on Microsoft’s AI ecosystem and how it supports enterprise transformation.

**Microsoft 365 Copilot & Microsoft Copilot**

- Mapping business processes to Copilot use cases
- Understanding differences between Copilot versions
- Capabilities of Copilot across Microsoft 365 apps
- Using Copilot Chat on web and mobile
- Knowing when to use Researcher or Analyst in Copilot
- Copilot extensibility: build, buy, or extend

**Microsoft Copilot Studio & Microsoft Graph**

- Creating customized AI experiences
- Using Microsoft Graph to ground AI in organizational data
- Understanding integrated AI solutions for risk mitigation and safety

**Azure AI Services**

- Mapping business use cases to Azure AI services
- Capabilities of Azure AI Vision, Azure AI Search, and Azure AI Foundry
- Matching AI models to business requirements
- Leveraging Azure AI for secure, scalable generative AI solutions

This section tests how well you can translate business needs into Microsoft AI solutions.

### 3. Identify an Implementation and Adoption Strategy for Microsoft AI (20–25%)

This domain evaluates your ability to lead AI adoption responsibly and sustainably.

**Responsible AI and Governance**

- Aligning AI strategy with Microsoft Responsible AI principles
- Establishing governance frameworks and policies
- Forming an AI council for oversight and cross-functional alignment
- Ensuring fairness, reliability, safety, privacy, inclusiveness, transparency, and accountability

**Adoption Planning**

- Building an AI adoption team
- Identifying common adoption barriers
- Establishing an AI champions program
- Managing data, security, privacy, and cost impacts

**Licensing and Cost Models**

- Microsoft 365 Copilot license types (monthly, pay-as-you-go, bundled)
- Azure AI subscription models (pay-as-you-go vs. prepaid)

This section emphasizes long-term success, not just initial deployment.
